Analysis: Global CO2 emissions set to rise 2% in 2017 after three-year ‘plateau’

China was the bigger emitter of CO2 in 2017 with 10.48 GtCO2. In 2017, there was little-to-no reductions in US emissions and a sizeable increase in Chinese emissions (0.33 GtCO2). India’s emissions increased a bit more slowly in 2017 to 2.47 GtCO2 from 2.43 GtCO2 in 2016, while the EU’s emissions have remained relatively flat since 2014 (3.47 GtCO2) and did not noticeably change in 2017 (3.48 GtCO2). The growth in emissions from 2016 to 2017 also more than doubled in the rest of the world from 0.15 to 0.3 GtCO2. Overall world CO2 emission in 2017 was 36.79 GtCO2 compared to 36.18 GtCO2 in 2016, with an increase of 0.6 GtCO2 where China contributed with more than half of this increase.
